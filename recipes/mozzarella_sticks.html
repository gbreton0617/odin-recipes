Mozzarella Stick Recipe

Mozzarella sticks have always held a special place in my heart, reminding me of cozy family gatherings and joyful, laughter-filled parties with friends. My fascination with them began in my grandmother's kitchen, where she skillfully turned simple ingredients into culinary magic. Inspired by her, I crafted my own recipe, enhancing the traditional snack with a few personal touches that elevate its flavors and textures.

Ingredients:

  • 12 sticks of mozzarella cheese (preferably whole milk mozzarella for creaminess)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 2 cups Italian seasoned breadcrumbs (for that extra herby flavor)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (adds a nice depth)
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder (for a kick of garlic)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Optional for serving:

  • Marianara sauce
  • Ranch dressing

Equipment:

  • Deep fryer or large heavy-bottomed pot
  • Paper towels for draining
  • Three shallow bowls

Instructions:

  1. Start by cutting the mozzarella into sticks if you haven’t bought pre-cut sticks. Freeze the sticks for at least two hours. This helps them hold their shape while frying.
  2. In the first bowl, mix the flour with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. In the second bowl, whisk the eggs and milk together until well combined.
  4. In the third bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan, garlic powder, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Roll each frozen mozzarella stick in the flour and shake off excess.
  6. Dip into the egg wash, then roll in the breadcrumb mixture. Press to coat well.
  7. For extra crispiness, double dip the sticks by repeating the egg wash and breadcrumb steps.
  8. Heat the oil in your deep fryer or pot to 375°F (190°C). Fry the mozzarella sticks in batches, careful not to overcrowd the pot, until golden brown and crispy, about 1 minute.
  9. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  10. Serve hot with bowls of marinara sauce and ranch dressing for dipping.

Personal Touch:

My grandmother always said that cooking isn't just about following a recipe—it's about putting a piece of your heart into the food. That’s why I like to add a sprinkle of love (and extra Parmesan!) when serving. These mozzarella sticks are more than just a snack; they're a warm reminder of my heritage and the joys of sharing good food with loved ones.

Enjoy making and eating these mozzarella sticks, and may they bring as much joy to your gatherings as they have to mine!
